GravRunner

 View on Itch.io

This is a game prototype to test the concept of speedrunning while messing around with gravity.
I took up this project to improve upon my level design and system design skills along with making a fun game to speedrun, somewhat akin to Carson or Portal.

Type: Personal project

Skill focus: level design, UX, mechanics design

Time spent: ~5 months

Engine and tools: Godot Engine, Blender

Core Pillars

SpeedRunning

Players experience the feeling of flying through the level both literally and figuritively

3D Movement

The player will have to think not just of his movement on the ground but also how zero-gravity affects movement in 3 directions

Learning Curve

The game is a skill which players can hone in on with time to improve their understanding.

Ideation

Lots of ideas were brainstormed and concepting and assessing all the options, Gravity  was chosen as the final topic.

The idea of switching ON/OFF gravity and tools which manipulate forces seemed to fit the theme the best.

Design Considerations

SpeedRunning

To emulate and promote the feeling of speedrunning, I focused on 3 specific parts of the player mechanics:

TIGHT CONTROLS

Players needed a lot of control over the way they move for them to want to play with the game's movement.

Allowing for changing directions at any time and an instant start/stop improves the control of the player over the character.

FEELING SPEEDY

Having the feeling of moving fast is essential to a game about speedrunning.

To emulate this, I made the character run really fast just by pressing any directional key, at 8.9 m/s.

Messing around with gravity and the PULL/PUSH ORBS can multiplicatively increase this speed.

Seamless gravity ON/OFF

To further promote the idea of going fast, the transitions between different gravity modes have been made seamless and instant, with high speeds in one mode providing additional benifits to the other.

Further, going from high speeds in zero-gravity to the normal mode maintains a lot of that high speed until you're not changing directions.
